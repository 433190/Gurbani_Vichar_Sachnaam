About Gurbani

Gurbani means the compositions of the Sikh Gurus and other enlightened saints included in the Guru Granth Sahib — the central scripture of Sikhism. It guides spiritual life, moral conduct, and the remembrance of the One Creator through sacred hymns and poetry.

ਸਤਿਨਾਮੁ ਕਰਤਾ ਪੁਰਖੁ ਨਿਰਭਉ ਨਿਰਵੈਰੁ ਅਕਾਲ ਮੂਰਤਿ ਅਜੂਨੀ ਸੈਭੰ ਗੁਰ ਪ੍ਰਸਾਦਿ

Transliteration (one commonly used): Satnam karta purakh nirbhao nirvair akal murat ajuni saibhang gurprasad.

Meaning (short): The Divine Name is Truth, the Creator being; without fear, without enmity; timeless in form, beyond birth and death, self-existent — known by the Guru's grace.

Key themes in Gurbani

  • Oneness of God (Ek Onakar) — unity beyond names and forms.
  • Remembering God (Simran) — constant remembrance through sacred Naam.
  • Humility and service (Seva) — serving others is a key spiritual practice.
  • Living honestly (Kirat Karni) — moral and truthful living.

Short Shabad (example)

These lines open the Guru Granth Sahib and capture the essential Sikh teaching: there is one timeless truth and creator.
